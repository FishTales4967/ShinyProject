The Loan Visualizer

This is intended to support prospective borrowers, who may not be financially savvy, in truly comprehending the long-term impact of their borrowing choices.

This app allows users to try various borrowing scenarios by entering into a simple user interface the amount of money they contemplate borrowing and the payment terms. The app calculates the repayment schedule and displays the schedule graphically, differentiating interest and principal components in the annual payments. A sample graphic is shown below.

The application

The app takes advantage of the mortgage function written by Thomas Girke ( Thomas Girke's source code at UCR.edu) This author made minor changes to the mortgage function for operation in the Shiny environment.

How it works

The function computes the payments on an annual basis for the life of the loan. The payments are broken out into principal and interest portions.

INPUTS to the mortgage function:

  • L, a numeric input, is the amount of the loan in dollars
  • I, a slider input, is the interest rate for the loan
  • P, a slider input, is the payment period in years

OUTPUTS: 1. Bar chart, with each bar showing the annual payment broken down into principal and interest companents. 2. Listed in the chart title, the total repayment amount for the loan, which is the total of the interest and principal repayments over the life of the loan. The annual payment is also listed.

The User Interface

The user enters the amount borrowed in the input box entitled “Loan Amount in $”. The sliders permit the user to input the interest rate of the loan and the number of years for loan repayment.Once these parameters are set, the user clicks the Update Graph button and the graph and titles reflect the payment schedule for the life of the loan.

The user can try various loan repayment scenarios for the amount of money they want to borrow and see the total cost and annual cost for each scenario.
